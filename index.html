Early Life

Warren Buffett was born in 1930 in Omaha, Nebraska. He started his education in Rose Hill Elementary School. He graduated in 1947 at Woodrow Wilson High School and had a quote in his yearbook that reads, "like math; a future stockbroker". He wanted to skipped college and go directly into business but he was turned down by his father. Buffett started to get interested in business at 7 years old, when he picked up a book at the library called One Thousand Ways to Make $1000. In his first business venture, Buffet went door to door selling chewing gum, Coca Cola bottles, and weekly magazines! He also worked at his grandfather's grocery store. He always found a way to make money. In High school, he use to deliver news paper, sell golf balls and detail cars! In 1947, Warren Buffett went to school in the University of Pennsylvania. He later transferred to the University of Nebraska when he was 19 and graduated with the Bachelor of Science in Business Adminitration .

Career

Warren Buffet started as a salesperson in 1951 to 1954. 1952, is when he became lifelong friend with Lorimer Davidson, Vice president of Geico. In 1970, is when he became a Chairman and CEO of Berkshire Hathaway Inc. Warren Buffet wanted to work for Wall Street but was advise not to by his father and Graham, a member of the board of Geico. Buffet took a public speaking course made by Dale Carneige, author of best selling book, How to Win Friends and Influence People . In 1973, he got stock from the Washington Post company and later owned stocked from the Coca Cola Company. Buffet became a billionaire in the late 1990s and started selling grade A shares with the price being $7,175 a share!
